### 質問 ###
How much water do I need to drink every day?

### 応答 (original) ###
At higher altitudes, the air is drier, and there's a risk of dehydration due to increased respiration rate and possible decreased thirst sensation. Travelers to high-altitude areas should increase their water intake to stay hydrated and help their bodies adapt to the new environment.
Dehydration occurs when your body loses more fluids than you take in, causing an insufficient amount of water and other fluids to carry out its normal functions. This condition can result from various factors, including:
1. **Not drinking enough water**: Failing to consume sufficient amounts of water throughout the day can lead to dehydration, especially in hot weather or during intense physical activity.
2. **Excessive sweating**: Activities that cause heavy sweating, such as vigorous exercise or working outdoors in hot conditions, can lead to significant fluid loss if not adequately replaced.
